To: Me, From: Me

June 7th, 2021 Roya Lotfi

“When I ask myself what I truly think will hold me back from achieving my goals, it is never the uncertainty of if I will be able to, but rather the question of whether I will be given the opportunity to prove that I can.” I wrote this quote as part of my personal statement for my application to the International Relations masters program at NYU, which I have now committed to and will be starting this Fall. Pursuing this graduate program will undoubtedly be the beginning to one of the most transformative phases of my life, a fact that both fills me with eagerness and existential dread. The latter stems from the fact that my prior quote is not entirely true - while I do believe that being given opportunities to prove I can achieve my goals is a factor that may greatly influence my success, I also know the action of actually proving and achieving those goals will have just as much an impact. And those two actions, unlike what I wrote in my personal statement, fall utterly and entirely on myself. So now, as I prepare to start this next chapter of my life that may lay the path to everything I’ve dreamed of doing, I must ask myself a question that may lead to a rather uncomfortable truth: what part of me is holding me back?
