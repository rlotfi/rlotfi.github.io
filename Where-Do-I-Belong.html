Where Do I Belong?

June 8th, 2021 Roya Lotfi

Today I listened to an episode titled “It’s Getting Harder to Vote in America” from the podcast “Today, Explained” by Vox Media. Ian Millhiser, a Vox News Senior Correspondent, was the featured interviewee on this episode that discussed recent voter suppression legislation in the U.S.. While I was listening to him discuss examples of the new legislation across multiple states, how members of both parties are responding to them, and the impacts they may have, I was struck by one emphatic thought: this is where he belongs. And that’s not in a stuck-up-rich-girl-to-the-poor-kid-who-got-a-scholarship-to-the-expensive-private-school “You don’t belong here” type of way. It’s in the way that Mr. Millhiser spoke with such expertise and clarity on the subject matter, with a repository or background knowledge that could have only stemmed from years of studying and experience, and with a JD from Duke University that gave him the credibility to speak on such matters. All of those factors manufactured in me the feeling that Mr. Millhiser was where he belonged: speaking about a complex and extremely important political issue on a respected podcast from a highly regarded news agency. And that, of course, led to the question of which this blog post is meant to ponder: where do I belong? Where will I belong?
